Multi-Quarter ATR · Merge Concentration Analysis

How often do deals merge ATR lines from multiple quarters — and where?

Every row in the source file is an opportunity line. COUNT_EXP_QTR is the number of quarters represented on the deal's lines that differ from the deal's booking quarter. COUNT_EXP_QTR > 1 ⇒ the deal merges ATR from multiple non-booking quarters (a "multi-quarter merge"). Source: MULTI QUARTER ATR.xlsx · 507,126 lines · 407,362 unique deals.

Headline: Multi-quarter merges are rare in count (2.8% of deals) but highly material in dollars — they carry 25.3% of total ATR ($2.93B of $11.59B). Concentration is heavily skewed toward Americas (68% of multi-qtr ATR), driven by US Public Sector, Global Enterprise, and SP segments.

So what?

Materiality

Multi-qtr merges are only 2.8% of deals but 25.3% of $ ATR. Average multi-qtr deal is ~9× larger than the average deal.

Concentration

Americas owns ~68% of multi-qtr ATR. Inside it, three pockets dominate: US PS (Federal), Global Enterprise, and the SP segments (Americas SP, APJ SP, EMEA SP) — all running 30–55% of segment ATR through multi-qtr merges.

Hot Spots

ASP WEB (76.5%), APJ_SP_JAPAN (51%), and STR_GES PREMIER (51%) run more than half their ATR through multi-qtr merges. A handful of mega-accounts (Microsoft US, Truist, Apple, Facebook) book >80% of their ATR this way.
