What changed — leadership insights

Dispute volume halved
−46%

Cases dropped from 10,844 to 5,879. Driven mainly by Americas (−60%) — earlier-cycle data threading and Account/Customer mapping fixes are reducing recurring dispute patterns.

Open backlog cut nearly in half
$73.4M → $39.6M

ATR Under Review dropped −46% YoY. Validation throughput improved despite a smaller team window.

SFC/NFP/PAYG nearly eliminated
−96%

$39.8M → $1.7M. Upstream classification at order capture is now catching these before they become disputes.

Asset Management cases rising
+50% ($80M → $120M)

The only major category moving the wrong direction. CX decommission & lifecycle data hygiene with Asset Managers is the focus area.

Collab cleanup more than doubled
+126% ($63.6M → $143.8M)

XaaS Replace/Modify ATR cleanup expanded materially — now multi-quarter (FY26 + FY27Q1) and proactive vs reactive.

Non-Renewable value jumped
+55% ($142M → $220M)

Case count is flat (443 → 422) but ATR per case nearly doubled — fewer, larger non-renewable disputes (Kaiser, Intel, Truist, DoS).
